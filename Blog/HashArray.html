Hashtables & ArrayLists

What's the difference between the two & how would you use them?

04 January, 2015 by Sandra Muller in Blog Posts


An ArrayList is a collection of items (they could be strings, ints, objects) while a Hashtable uses Key/Value pairs, represented as (key, value).

For accessing an ArrayList, you use an index number, which is automatically generated and starts at 0 incrementing by 1 for each item in the ArrayList, to manipulate the value. When you are adding values to a Hashtable you use a key, which is what you will use to manipulate the value linked to that key.
