Padding, Borders & Margins

What is the difference between the three and how would you use them?

04 January, 2015 by Sandra Muller in Blog Posts


A boxmodel image of content in HTML/CSS



image from http://guistuff.com/css/css_boxmodel.html



All visual elements within a page are considered to be boxes, each with its own padding, border and margin. You may be quite familiar with borders and margins from working with documents or sheets in word/excel. Similarly you can change the border to be black and thick. The margin allows you to set what spacing you would like to have between the content and other content. When you are styling your content you can set the background colour and this incorrporates the padding. Bellow are a few demonstartions. I have included the styling in my HTML rather than CSS, but ultimately styling through CSS is a better practice.


Plain content no styling

background colour changed to blue - #BDEDFF

padding adjusted - padding: 18px; background-color: #BDEDFF

now a border has been applied - padding: 18px; background-color: #BDEDFF; border: 1px solid black

now the margins have been set - padding: 18px; background-color: #BDEDFF; border: 1px solid black; margin: 30px



I hope this visual demonstartion has been helpful. I learn better when seeing something in action ;)
